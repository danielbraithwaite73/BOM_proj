Living The Gospel

One of the central purposes of The Book of Mormon is to teach the gospel of Jesus Christ. The gospel of Jesus Christ includes faith, repentance, baptism by immersion, receiving the Holy Ghost by the laying on of hands, and enduring to the end. As seen on the homepage, one of the promises the Lord makes with living the gospel of Jesus Christ is being held guiltless (3 Nephi 27:16). A more common promise the Lord makes with living the gospel of Jesus Christ is salvation. I find that this is the most important promise made in The Book of Mormon since it has to do with our eternal welfare.

To emphasis its importance and to show its frequency, here are a few of the times of when salvation is promised in association with living the gospel of Jesus Christ:

"And whoso believeth in me, and is baptized, the same shall be saved; and they are they who shall inherit the kingdom of God." (3 Nephi 11:33)

"And whosoever will hearken unto my words and repenteth and is baptized, the same shall be saved. Search the prophets, for many there be that testify of these things." (3 Nephi 23:5)

"And whoso taketh upon him my name, and endureth to the end, the same shall be saved at the last day." (3 Nephi 27:6)
